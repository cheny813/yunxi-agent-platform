# Nutrition Recipe Skill

## Overview

This skill provides expertise in campus meal nutrition planning, including recipe composition, nutrition scoring, dietary standard compliance, and food ingredient analysis.

## Key Capabilities

### 1. Recipe Composition
- Generate weekly/daily meal plans based on age group calorie standards
- Ensure meal structure: staple + protein + vegetable per meal
- Maintain food diversity: ≥5 types per meal, ≥12 types per day, covering 8 major food categories

### 2. Nutrition Assessment
- Calorie compliance: target within 80%-120% of recommended intake
- Macronutrient ratio: protein 12-15%, fat 20-30%, carbohydrate 55-65%
- Micronutrient adequacy: calcium, iron, zinc, vitamin A/B1/B2/C

### 3. Food Categories
Eight major categories must be covered in meal plans:
- Cereals and tubers (谷薯类)
- Vegetables (蔬菜类)
- Fruits (水果类)
- Livestock and poultry meat (畜禽肉类)
- Aquatic products (水产类)
- Eggs (蛋类)
- Dairy (奶类)
- Soy products (豆制品)

### 4. Age Group Standards (daily kcal)
| Age Group | Daily Calorie | Breakfast | Lunch | Dinner |
|-----------|--------------|-----------|-------|--------|
| Preschool (3-6) | 1000-1200 | 280-340 | 370-450 | 280-340 |
| Primary Low (6-9) | 1200-1500 | 350-420 | 450-550 | 350-420 |
| Primary High (10-12) | 1500-1800 | 420-500 | 550-660 | 420-500 |
| Junior High (13-15) | 2000-2400 | 550-650 | 700-850 | 550-650 |
| Senior High (16-18) | 2200-2600 | 600-720 | 800-960 | 600-720 |

Meal ratio: Breakfast 28-32%, Lunch 35-40%, Dinner 28-32%, Snacks 5-8%

## Scoring Dimensions
| Dimension | Weight | Target |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| Energy Supply | 30% | 80%-120% of recommended |
| Macronutrients | 25% | Protein 12-15%, Fat 20-30%, Carb 55-65% |
| Micronutrients | 20% | Ca/Fe/Zn/VitA/B1/B2/C adequate |
| Food Diversity | 15% | ≥5/meal, ≥12/day, 8 categories |
| Meal Structure | 10% | Staple + protein + vegetable per meal |

## Optimization Strategies
- Low calorie → increase staples or high-calorie ingredients
- Low protein → increase meat, eggs, soy products
- Low vitamins → increase vegetables and fruits
- High fat → reduce fried foods, increase steaming/boiling
- Low diversity → add side dishes
